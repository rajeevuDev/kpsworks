Chapters - 7

1. Which of the following words describe the lady best?

(a) imposing
(d) a great eater
(e) talkative

2. Mention two good qualities you notice in the author.

The author is polite and patient.

3. List the things in sequence as ordered by the lady in the restaurant.

The lady ordered salmon, caviar, asparagus, ice cream, coffee, and a peach.

4. What advice did the lady keep on giving to the author?

The lady kept telling the author to eat less and avoid heavy food.

5. Why did the lady tell the author 'you're quite a humorist'? What did she mean?

She said this because the author joked that he would skip dinner. She meant he was being funny.

6. 'We waited for the asparagus to be cooked. Panic seized me'. Why did panic seize the author?

The author panicked because he was afraid he didn’t have enough money to pay for everything the lady ordered.

Chapters - 8

1. Who was admitted to the hospital and why?

The narrator’s father was admitted to the hospital because he had malaria.

2. What did the writer think about the dressing-gown hanging on the bathroom door?

The writer thought the dressing-gown hanging on the bathroom door was his father. This made him feel scared and anxious because he believed his father had come back from the hospital.

3. What made the writer long for his father?

The writer longed for his father because he felt lonely and scared while staying in the bungalow without him. The dark and silent night heightened his anxiety, making him wish for his father’s presence.

4. Why was the writer frightened and what did he do to seek protection?

The writer was frightened due to the dark night, the sounds of the jungle, and the thought of ghosts. To seek protection, he fled to the sweeper-boy’s room, where he expressed his fear and sat beside him for comfort.

5. Why did the woman advise the writer to keep away from the sweeper-boy?

The woman advised the writer to keep away from the sweeper-boy because she considered him unclean and a servant, suggesting that he should only play with her children.

Chapters - 9

1. What is the best way to guard against the threat of extinction of wild animals?

The best way to guard against the threat of extinction of wild animals is by creating nature reserves. These protected areas help preserve the habitats of endangered species, allowing them to live and thrive without human interference.

2. Why does man kill whales and sharks?

Man kills whales and sharks mainly for their meat and fat. Some people also hunt them for specific dishes, like shark curry, or for products derived from them, which can lead to overfishing and threaten their populations.

3. Where are lions found in large numbers in the world?

Lions are found in large numbers primarily in Africa. They used to be widespread across Asia as well, but their population has significantly decreased in that region.

4. Give reasons why Giant pandas are threatened with extinction.

Giant pandas are threatened with extinction for several reasons: their forest habitat in southwest China is fragmented, meaning that their living areas are broken into smaller, isolated parts. Additionally, poaching remains a serious threat to their survival.

5. Wildlife will not get any support from the very poor people of the world. Why?

Wildlife will not get support from very poor people because they often prioritize their immediate needs, such as food and shelter, over conservation efforts. When faced with poverty, protecting animals can seem less important compared to survival.

6. What is the role of the WWF in protecting wildlife?

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) plays a crucial role in protecting wildlife by raising awareness about endangered species and their habitats. It works on conservation projects, promotes sustainable practices, and advocates for laws to protect wildlife and biodiversity.

7. How is humanity a victim of its own destructive way of life?

Humanity is a victim of its own destructive way of life because excessive consumption and habitat destruction lead to the extinction of wildlife, which harms ecosystems that provide essential services like clean air, water, and food. As species disappear, it threatens the balance of nature, ultimately affecting human survival.

Chapters - 10

1. What did Anshi dream of becoming?

Anshi dreamed of becoming a great singer.

2. What features of Anshi's physique have been described in the story?

Anshi had waist-length, dark-black hair and eyes that were the color of almonds.

3. Give a description of the bird Anshi saw near the water pump.

The bird Anshi saw was a Malabar Whistling Thrush. It was black with beautiful blue feathers on its head and shoulders. It had a lovely whistling song that captivated Anshi.

4. What did the bird teach Anshi and why?

The bird taught Anshi how to sing better. It wanted her to help it quench its thirst with water, and in return, it offered to give her singing lessons.

5. What did Anshi do to overcome her fear while singing before the students?

To overcome her fear, Anshi closed her eyes and imagined that her classmates were a flock of birds, just like the ones she sang to during her practice with the thrush.

6. How did Anshi feel when she stood on the stage to sing?

Anshi felt very nervous when she stood on the stage. Her palms were wet with fear, and she had a dry throat.

7. What did Anshi win? What did she say after her performance?

Anshi won the singing contest and received a prize of six hundred rupees. After her performance, she said, "Be kind to animals, for kindness will surely be returned by them."
